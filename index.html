The Bastille — Symbol of Freedom

by Akshara, Akshita & Yaalini

The Bastille was a medieval fortress in Paris that became infamous as a state prison. It symbolized the absolute power of the French monarchy. On 14 July 1789, it was stormed by revolutionaries, marking a turning point in history. Today, it represents the triumph of liberty over oppression.

About the Bastille

Built in the late 1300s, the Bastille served as a fortress to defend Paris. By the 17th century, it became a prison where political opponents of the monarchy were held, often without trial. Its towering walls and reputation for secrecy made it a hated symbol of tyranny.

The fall of the Bastille inspired people around the world and became a universal symbol of the fight against oppression.

Bastille Day

Every year on 14 July, France celebrates Bastille Day to honor the storming of the Bastille in 1789. It is a national holiday marked by military parades, fireworks, concerts, and gatherings that celebrate liberty, unity, and democracy.

The largest celebration takes place in Paris, with a grand military parade along the Champs-Élysées attended by thousands, including the French President. Around the world, French communities also celebrate this day as a reminder of freedom and human rights.
